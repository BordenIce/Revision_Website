Chapter 1 - Reasons for System Development

This topic covers...

  • Software Crisis
  • Factors Affecting System Development
  • Explain the impact of “Software Crisis”
  • Explain the need for software systems that meet the needs of organisations and/or individuals
  • Explain the main factors affecting systems development:
    • The user needs
    • Time
    • Cost
  • Understand that a computer system consists of a user interface, processes, and data
  • Describe the roles of the following during systems development:
    • The systems analyst
    • The project manager
    • The programmer

The development of software systems has become an essential component in meeting the evolving demands of both organizations and individuals. Historically, the term "Software Crisis" emerged during the 1960s to describe the growing challenges in software development, such as budget overruns, missed deadlines, and systems that failed to meet user requirements.

This crisis highlighted the increasing complexity of software systems and the difficulty of managing large-scale projects effectively. As technology advanced, the need for more sophisticated and reliable software became apparent, pushing the industry to adopt better practices and methodologies.

Effective system development hinges on the seamless collaboration of all stakeholders, ensuring that the final product aligns with both user expectations and project constraints. This collaboration, coupled with a deep understanding of the system's requirements and challenges, helps to mitigate risks such as budget overruns and delays, ultimately leading to a successful software solution that enhances productivity and meets the specific needs of its users.

Factors affecting system development are crucial in determining the success or failure of a project. One of the most significant factors is understanding user needs. Systems must be designed to meet the specific requirements of their users, whether they are individuals or organizations. Failing to do so can result in software that is difficult to use or fails to deliver the expected outcomes. Another critical factor is time. Timely delivery is essential in ensuring that the software remains relevant and useful in a rapidly changing technological landscape. Delays can lead to missed opportunities and reduced competitiveness.

A computer system typically consists of three main components: a user interface, processes, and data. The user interface is the point of interaction between the user and the system, making it crucial for the system's usability. Processes are the actions the system performs to achieve specific tasks, while data represents the information the system manipulates.

During system development, several roles are critical to ensuring the project’s success. The systems analyst is responsible for understanding and documenting the user’s requirements, the project manager oversees the project’s progress and ensures that it stays on track, and the programmer writes the code that brings the system to life. Each of these roles is essential in delivering a system that meets the needs of its users while being delivered on time and within budget.

The Systems Analyst

The systems analyst plays a critical role in the early stages of systems development. Their primary responsibility is to bridge the gap between the business requirements and the technical implementation. They work closely with stakeholders, including end-users, to gather and document the functional and non-functional requirements of the system. The systems analyst also analyzes existing systems, identifies areas for improvement, and designs solutions that meet the business objectives. They create detailed specifications and diagrams that guide the development team, ensuring that the final product aligns with the user needs and overall project goals.

The Project Manager

The project manager is responsible for overseeing the entire systems development process from inception to completion. Their role involves planning, executing, and closing the project while managing resources, time, and budget. The project manager coordinates the work of the development team, monitors progress, and addresses any issues that arise to keep the project on track. They also serve as the main point of contact between the stakeholders and the development team, ensuring clear communication and alignment of expectations. Risk management is a key aspect of their role, as they anticipate potential problems and implement strategies to mitigate them.

The Programmer

The programmer, also known as the software developer, is responsible for writing the actual code that makes the system function. They take the detailed specifications provided by the systems analyst and translate them into executable software. This role requires a deep understanding of programming languages, algorithms, and software design principles. Programmers also conduct testing and debugging to ensure the code is error-free and meets the specified requirements. They work closely with other team members, such as designers and testers, to integrate various components and ensure the system operates smoothly as a whole. The programmer’s work is crucial in turning the conceptual designs into a functional software product.
